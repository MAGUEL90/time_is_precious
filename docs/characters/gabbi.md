# Gabbi - Unique NPC Character Bible

Status: Draft reference  
Last updated: 2026-06-05  
Game: Time is Precious

This document preserves the current remembered direction for Gabbi so the character does not need to be re-explained repeatedly.

## 1. Character Classification

Gabbi is a **Unique NPC**, not a regular worker.

She belongs to the story / relationship layer of the game, not the routine workforce layer.

### Core rule

Gabbi should **not** be assigned to normal repeatable labor such as gathering, crafting, hauling, farming, or production jobs.

If Gabbi helps the player, her help should happen through:

- quest support
- event support
- story involvement
- special help tied to specific situations
- guidance for player decision-making
- social connection to ordinary citizens

This keeps her available for dialogue, story events, emotional beats, and key quest moments.

## 2. Core Role

Gabbi is currently the reference model for a Unique NPC.

Her main roles are:

- childhood friend of the player
- early guide
- social bridge to lower-class citizens
- story support character
- emotional anchor for the city's human cost

She helps the player understand that rebuilding the city is not only about money, production, or efficiency.
It is also about people, trust, poverty, debt, care, and whether the new city avoids repeating the failures of the old one.

## 3. Relationship to the Player

Gabbi is the player's childhood friend.

This makes her emotionally important from the beginning of the game. She can speak to the player more directly than most NPCs because she knew the player before the city's collapse became the central problem.

Her relationship with the player should feel familiar, honest, and grounded.

She can:

- tease the player lightly
- challenge selfish or careless decisions
- encourage the player when the city feels overwhelming
- remind the player of ordinary citizens' suffering
- act as a trusted early companion without becoming a controllable worker

## 4. Personality Direction

Gabbi is:

- cheerful
- stubbornly hopeful
- clever
- sometimes moody
- socially aware
- emotionally grounded

Her optimism is **not naive**.

She has seen poverty closely, so when she hopes for a better city, that hope comes from pain, memory, and survival rather than childish innocence.

She wants the city to recover, but she does not fully trust prosperity that is built without care.

## 5. Background Direction

Gabbi comes from a lower-class family shaped by:

- debt
- illness
- instability
- lack of access to proper care
- pressure from the city's unequal prosperity

Her mother died from sickness after the family could not afford proper care.

Her father may be rough, irresponsible, trapped in debt, or emotionally damaged by the family's hardship. This part is still a flexible direction and should not be locked too rigidly until the story is developed further.

This background explains why Gabbi cares deeply about ordinary citizens and why she naturally becomes a bridge between the player and lower-class people.

## 6. Story Function

Gabbi should help make the city feel human.

She is useful for:

- early-game guidance
- introducing lower-class citizen problems
- showing the consequences of debt and neglect
- grounding the player's rebuilding mission emotionally
- creating story tension when economic decisions hurt vulnerable citizens
- helping the player notice problems that pure city statistics may hide

She should not simply praise the player. She should sometimes disagree when the player's decisions make the city richer but less humane.

## 7. Gameplay Function

Gabbi can support gameplay through non-routine systems.

Recommended uses:

### Early Guide

Gabbi can introduce:

- basic city problems
- early NPC needs
- lower-class districts
- simple quests
- the difference between regular workers and unique NPCs

### Social Bridge

Gabbi can connect the player to:

- poor families
- indebted citizens
- sick or neglected residents
- workers who distrust authority
- citizens who need help before they are willing to cooperate

### Emotional Anchor

Gabbi can react to:

- food shortages
- unpaid wages
- lack of shelter
- unstable city satisfaction
- decisions that prioritize profit over people

### Special Support

Gabbi may help during specific events, but this should be scripted or limited.

Examples:

- guiding the player to someone who needs help
- warning about unrest in poorer areas
- helping calm citizens during a story event
- providing context about a family's condition
- unlocking a quest route through trust

## 8. Design Boundaries

To protect Gabbi's role, avoid these directions:

- Do not turn her into a normal worker.
- Do not make her disappear for long routine assignments.
- Do not make her just a tutorial robot.
- Do not make her blindly supportive of every player decision.
- Do not reduce her background to tragedy only; she still needs warmth, agency, and personality.
- Do not make her replace city systems; she should interpret and humanize them.

## 9. Connection to Game Themes

Gabbi supports the main themes of Time is Precious:

- Time is valuable because people suffer when help comes too late.
- Money is powerful, but it can either rebuild or exploit.
- Delegation and systems matter, but people are not just numbers.
- A city that ignores ordinary citizens will collapse again.
- True rebuilding means creating stability, trust, and dignity, not just wealth.
