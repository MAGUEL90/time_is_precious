# Item Description Style Guide

## Project

**Time is Precious**

This guide defines the writing style for item descriptions in the game.
The goal is to make every item feel like it belongs to an ancient village world inspired by Mesopotamia, while still being easy for modern players to understand.

## Core Style

**Mesopotamian-inspired English**

The descriptions should feel ancient, earthy, warm, and slightly humorous.
They should not feel like formal museum text, generic RPG text, or heavy medieval fantasy.

## Tone

Use this tone:

- Ancient village feel
- Simple and readable English
- Earthy and practical
- Warm, witty, and slightly playful
- Humorous, but not pure parody
- Grounded in work, food, shelter, trade, survival, and village life

Avoid this tone:

- Too formal
- Too stiff
- Too modern-slang heavy
- Too medieval
- Too difficult to understand
- Too joke-heavy until the world feels unserious

## Vocabulary Direction

Prefer words and imagery connected to ancient village life:

- clay
- barley
- reed
- bronze
- canal
- scribe
- tablet
- mudbrick
- village
- sun
- goat
- tax
- granary
- river clay
- dusty ground
- baked earth
- date palm
- caravan
- temple storehouse

## Medieval Words

Avoid overusing words like:

- ye
- thou
- thee
- thy

These words feel more medieval English than Mesopotamian. They may be used rarely if a specific item needs a fantasy flavor, but they should not become the main voice of the game.

## Humor Rules

Humor should be light and grounded.
Good joke subjects:

- goats
- taxes
- heat
- hungry workers
- poor planning
- village gossip
- tired scribes
- stubborn merchants
- suspicious food

Bad joke direction:

- too modern internet humor
- too many memes
- jokes that break the game world too hard
- jokes that make serious survival needs feel meaningless

## Description Length

Most item descriptions should be **1-2 short sentences**.

Good structure:

1. Explain what the item is.
2. Add a small flavor line or joke.

Example:

> A lump of river clay, soft and full of promise. Shape it well, or it shall remain a proud little blob.

## Example Style

### Barley Bread

A tough little bread made from barley. Dry enough to humble a king, useful enough to keep a worker standing.

### Clay Cup

A simple cup shaped from river clay. Holds water, beer, or the tears of a scribe after tax season.

### Bronze Hoe

A farming tool of honest bronze. Not glamorous, but neither is starving.

### Reed Basket

Woven from river reeds. Carries grain, fish, and occasionally someone's poor decisions.

## Final Rule

The player should understand the item immediately, smile a little, and feel that the world has its own voice.
