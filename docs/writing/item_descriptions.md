# Item Descriptions

## Writing Style

These item descriptions follow the **Mesopotamian-inspired English** style defined in:

`docs/writing/item_description_style_guide.md`

The tone should be ancient, earthy, readable, warm, and lightly humorous.

---

## Butcher's Cut

**File:** `butchers_cut.tres`

A hearty cut of meat from the village butcher. Fresh enough for supper, suspicious enough to ask no further questions.

## Clay Lump

**File:** `clay_lump.tres`

A lump of river clay, soft and full of promise. Shape it well, or it shall remain a proud little blob.

## Fresh Loaf

**File:** `fresh_loaf.tres`

A warm loaf baked for hungry hands. Simple food, but even kings stop complaining when their stomach is loud.

## Orchard Apple

**File:** `orchard_apple.tres`

A crisp fruit from the village orchard. Sweet, round, and far less troublesome than most people.

## Pan-Fried Egg

**File:** `pan_fried_egg.tres`

An egg fried hot over the village fire. Small meal, great comfort, and proof that chickens still serve the economy.

## Roasted Drumstick

**File:** `roasted_drumstick.tres`

A roasted leg fit for a tired worker. Greasy fingers are a small price for renewed hope.

## Simple Clothes

**File:** `simple_clothes.tres`

Plain clothes for honest labor. They will not impress nobles, but they will keep the sun from judging too harshly.

## Stone

**File:** `stone.tres`

A common stone from the dusty ground. Not exciting, but civilization was not built from compliments.

## Straw Bundle

**File:** `straw_bundle.tres`

A bundle of dry straw tied for work and shelter. Useful for roofs, bedding, and pretending the goats will not eat it.

## Sun-Dried Mudbrick

**File:** `sun_dried_mudbrick.tres`

A mudbrick hardened under the cruel sun. Strong enough for walls, weak enough to fear rain like a merchant fears taxes.

## Village Cheese

**File:** `village_cheese.tres`

A humble cheese made by village hands. Smells bold, tastes better, and may start a debate at the table.

## Water Jar

**File:** `water_jar.tres`

A clay jar filled with precious water. Guard it well; the desert does not offer refunds.

## Wet Mudbrick

**File:** `wet_mudbrick.tres`

A fresh mudbrick still soft from shaping. Give it sun and patience, not footsteps and poor planning.
